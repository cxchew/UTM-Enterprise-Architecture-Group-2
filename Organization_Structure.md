# UTM Organization Structure

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the organizational structure of Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM) as it relates to the Academic Advising and Progress Tracker system implementation. The structure defines key stakeholders, their roles, and their relationships within the enterprise architecture.

## Organizational Hierarchy

### 1. University Leadership
- **Vice Chancellor**
  - Overall university governance and strategic direction
  - Final authority on major system implementations
  
- **Deputy Vice Chancellor (Academic & International)**
  - Oversees academic programs and policies
  - Primary sponsor for academic systems
  - Approves academic advising policies and procedures

- **Deputy Vice Chancellor (Research & Innovation)**
  - Manages research programs and innovation initiatives
  - Stakeholder for student progress tracking related to research

### 2. Academic Leadership

#### Deans of Faculties
- **Faculty of Engineering**
- **Faculty of Science**
- **Faculty of Computing**
- **Faculty of Built Environment and Surveying**
- **Faculty of Social Sciences and Humanities**
- **Faculty of Management**
- **Faculty of Education**

**Responsibilities:**
- Faculty-level academic governance
- Academic program oversight
- Faculty-specific advising policy implementation
- Resource allocation for advising services

#### Heads of Departments
- Departmental academic management
- Course and curriculum coordination
- Direct oversight of academic advisors
- Student performance monitoring at department level

### 3. Student Affairs Division

#### Dean of Students
- Student welfare and development
- Overall student services coordination
- Academic advising program oversight

#### Academic Advising Unit
- **Head of Academic Advising**
  - Manages university-wide advising programs
  - Primary system owner for Academic Advising and Progress Tracker
  - Develops advising standards and best practices

- **Senior Academic Advisors**
  - Faculty-level advising coordination
  - Training and mentoring of advisors
  - Policy implementation

- **Academic Advisors**
  - Direct student advising and counseling
  - Primary system users
  - Student progress monitoring and intervention

### 4. Registrar's Office

#### Registrar
- Academic records management
- Enrollment and registration oversight
- Academic calendar coordination
- Data custodian for student records

#### Assistant Registrars
- Student information system management
- Academic record keeping
- Grade processing and transcript generation
- Integration with advising systems

### 5. Information Technology Services (ITS)

#### Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- University IT strategy and governance
- Enterprise architecture oversight
- System integration and security

#### IT Development & Support Teams
- **Application Development Team**
  - System development and customization
  - Integration implementation
  - Technical support for advising system

- **Database Administration Team**
  - Database management and optimization
  - Data integrity and security
  - Backup and recovery

- **IT Security Team**
  - Information security policies
  - Access control management
  - Compliance monitoring

- **IT Support Desk**
  - User support and training
  - Issue resolution and ticketing
  - Documentation management

### 6. Academic Staff

#### Faculty Members / Lecturers
- Course instruction and assessment
- Academic mentoring
- Input to advising recommendations
- Early warning identification

#### Course Coordinators
- Course management and oversight
- Performance data provision
- Collaboration with advisors on student issues

### 7. Students

#### Undergraduate Students
- Primary beneficiaries of advising services
- Self-service portal users
- Recipients of academic guidance and support

#### Graduate Students
- Research progress tracking users
- Specialized advising needs
- Milestone tracking and compliance

## Organizational Relationships

### Reporting Lines
```
Vice Chancellor
├── Deputy VC (Academic & International)
│   ├── Deans of Faculties
│   │   └── Heads of Departments
│   │       └── Faculty Members
│   ├── Dean of Students
│   │   └── Head of Academic Advising
│   │       ├── Senior Academic Advisors
│   │       └── Academic Advisors
│   └── Registrar
│       └── Assistant Registrars
└── Chief Information Officer (CIO)
    └── IT Development & Support Teams
```

### Collaborative Relationships

#### Academic Advising Ecosystem
- **Advisors ↔ Students**: Primary advising relationship
- **Advisors ↔ Faculty**: Course performance and academic concerns
- **Advisors ↔ Registrar**: Academic records and enrollment data
- **Advisors ↔ IT Services**: System support and enhancements

#### System Governance
- **Steering Committee**
  - Deputy VC (Academic & International) - Chair
  - Dean of Students
  - Selected Faculty Deans
  - Registrar
  - CIO
  - Head of Academic Advising
  
  **Purpose**: Strategic direction, policy approval, resource allocation

- **Technical Working Group**
  - IT Development Lead
  - Academic Advising Representatives
  - Registrar's Office Representatives
  - Faculty IT Coordinators
  
  **Purpose**: Technical requirements, implementation planning, issue resolution

## Change Management Structure

### Decision Authority Levels

**Level 1 - Strategic (Requires Steering Committee Approval)**
- Major policy changes
- Significant system enhancements
- Budget allocations over threshold
- New module implementations

**Level 2 - Tactical (Requires Working Group Approval)**
- Process modifications
- Integration with new systems
- Training program changes
- Minor enhancements

**Level 3 - Operational (Department Level Authority)**
- Daily operational procedures
- User support issues
- Report generation
- Routine maintenance

## Communication Channels

### Formal Communication
- Monthly Steering Committee meetings
- Bi-weekly Technical Working Group meetings
- Quarterly stakeholder updates
- Annual system review

### Operational Communication
- Email distribution lists by role
- System notification broadcasts
- Help desk ticketing system
- Training workshops and webinars

## Stakeholder Engagement

### Primary Stakeholders
- Academic Advisors (High engagement, daily users)
- Students (High engagement, primary beneficiaries)
- Registrar's Office (High engagement, data integration)
- IT Services (High engagement, system support)

### Secondary Stakeholders
- Faculty Deans (Medium engagement, policy input)
- Department Heads (Medium engagement, oversight)
- Faculty Members (Medium engagement, information consumers)

### Tertiary Stakeholders
- University Leadership (Low engagement, strategic oversight)
- External Auditors (Low engagement, compliance review)
- Accreditation Bodies (Low engagement, standards compliance)

## Conclusion

This organizational structure provides clear lines of authority, responsibility, and communication for the Academic Advising and Progress Tracker system. The structure ensures:
- Clear ownership and accountability
- Effective decision-making processes
- Appropriate stakeholder engagement
- Sustainable system governance

The structure supports both the strategic goals of the university and the operational needs of academic advising services, ensuring that the system delivers value to all stakeholders.
